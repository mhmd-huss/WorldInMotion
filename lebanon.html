Lebanon

A land of mountains, history, and unmatched cultural richness.

Overview

Lebanon is a Middle Eastern country known for its breathtaking mountains, Mediterranean coastline, deep history, and vibrant multicultural identity. Blending Phoenician heritage, Arab culture, and European influences, Lebanon remains one of the most culturally diverse nations in the region.

Culture

Lebanese culture is rooted in hospitality, tradition, and community. Music, dabke dance, literature, and storytelling all play important roles. The country's diverse religious landscape contributes to its rich celebrations and holidays. Lebanon is also known for its artistic contributions and poetic legacy.

Traditional Food

Lebanese cuisine is beloved around the world for its freshness, flavor, and variety. Classic dishes include hummus, tabbouleh, kibbeh, manakish, and shawarma. Meals often feature olive oil, fresh vegetables, herbs, and grilled meats. Lebanese sweets such as baklava and knefeh are also internationally famous.

Famous Landmarks

Baalbek Ruins

One of the grandest Roman temple complexes in the world.

Raouché (Pigeon Rocks)

A famous natural rock formation along Beirut’s coastline.

Jeita Grotto

A stunning system of interconnected limestone caves and underground lakes.
