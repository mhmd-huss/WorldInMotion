Russia

A vast nation of contrasts, history, and cultural richness.

Overview

Russia is the largest country in the world, stretching across Eastern Europe and northern Asia. Known for its diverse landscapes—from snowy Siberia to vibrant cities like Moscow and St. Petersburg— Russia has a long history, deep traditions, and a unique cultural identity influenced by Slavic roots and centuries of empire.

Culture

Russian culture values literature, ballet, classical music, and the arts. Writers such as Tolstoy and Dostoevsky shaped world literature, while the Bolshoi Ballet remains globally admired. Traditional customs include folk dances, colorful clothing, and celebrations rooted in both Orthodox Christianity and ancient Slavic traditions.

Traditional Food

Russian cuisine is hearty and comforting, shaped by cold winters and local ingredients. Popular dishes include borscht (beet soup), pelmeni (dumplings), blini (crepes), and beef stroganoff. Tea culture is very strong, and meals often include rye bread, pickled vegetables, and warming soups.

Famous Landmarks

Saint Basil’s Cathedral

An architectural icon in Moscow, known for its colorful onion domes.

The Kremlin

A historic fortress and the political heart of Russia.

Lake Baikal

The world’s deepest and oldest freshwater lake, famous for its clarity.
